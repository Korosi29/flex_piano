The Major Scale

The major scale is one of the many scales we have in music but what makes the major scale special is the fact that all our diatonic chords(Do Re Mi Fah....) are built upon this scale. A scale is the arrangement of specific notes in a specific interval and is played sequentially. I hope that makes sense.

The major scale consist of the following intervals: W-W-H-W-W-W-H W stands for Whole Tone or Tone While H stands for Half Step or Semitone. For example key F major scale

The F major has 6 whites keys and one black key. Observe the interval. It says Tone-Tone-Semitone-Tone-Tone-Tone-Semitone or W-W-H-W-W-W-H. With this formula, we can now play the major scale of any key without knowing it first hand.

Solfa Notation

You might ask what is solfa notation (also Solfeggio) and what does it has to do with the major scale? Well, the Solfa notation is a system that uses syllables (do, re, mi, fa, sol, la, ti) to represent the major scale notes that we have talked about. For example in the key of C.

It is also possible to hum the major scale of any key with your voice given that you can hum the tonic (do) of that key.

Number Notation

The number notation (Nashville Number System) is another system just like the Solfa notation but this one uses numbers to represent each degree. For example in the key of C.

From now on, if any of these systems is used, it shouldn't be confusing. The solfa - Do can also mean 1 or major on the tonic note of that key. They might all mean the same thing depending on the use case.

Understanding the major scale and it's composition is so super important as almost everything in other lessons will still point back to it.
