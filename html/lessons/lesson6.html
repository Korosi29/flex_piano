The Pentatonic Scale

The pentatonic scale is one of the many scales we have in music. It isn’t particularly special on its own, but it’s widely used across many genres such as jazz, R&B, and gospel. Because it contains fewer notes and produces less dissonance, we can use it in almost any musical context. (Note that we have major and minor pentatonic but we are only referring to the major pentatonic here)

Looking at the major scale in our previous chapter, the pentatonic scale consist of the following degree: 1st, 2nd, 3rd, 5th and the 6th degree or rather if you prefer Do, Re, Mi, Sol, La.

Usage

You might ask what are the instances to use the pentatonic scale? The pentatonic scale can be used for a variety of purposes. To mention a few:

  • Improvisation
  • Riff and melody creation
  • Worship grooves and fills
  • African traditional music

If you do not know anything about improvisation or any of the listed terms, that's okay. You will in our subsequent lessons

Now that you have a basic understanding of what the pentatonic scale is, you can apply it in your playing right away to feel how it sounds
