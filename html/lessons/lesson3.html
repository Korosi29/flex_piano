What is an Interval

An interval is the distance between two musical notes. Just like measuring the space between two points on a ruler, intervals help us measure the space between two notes on the piano or any other instrument. Intervals are one of the most important building blocks of music because they define the relationships between notes, create melodies, and form chords.

Types of Intervals

Intervals are categorized based on how far apart the two notes are from each other.

1. Melodic vs. Harmonic Intervals
  • Melodic Interval - When the two notes are played one after the other (like a melody).
  • Harmonic Interval - When the two notes are played at the same time (like a chord).

Both types of intervals are used in music to create different effects. Melodic intervals form the basis of tunes, while harmonic intervals create depth and harmony in chords.

Major intervals sound bright and happy, while minor intervals sound darker or sadder. We have other intervals such as Augmented and Diminished intervals but we won't talk about them because we will see them in other topics such as "Chords" in our subsequent lessons

Why Are Intervals Important for You to Learn?

Intervals are essential in music for several reasons:

Building Scales

Scales are made up of specific intervals. The major scale(we shall see in our subsequent lesson) follows a pattern of whole and half steps, while the minor scale has different intervals that give it a darker sound.

Creating Chords

Chords are built using stacked intervals. For example, a major chord(we shall see in our subsequent lesson) consists of a major third and a perfect fifth (C - E - G).

Recognizing and Playing Melodies

Understanding intervals helps musicians play songs by ear, as they can recognize the distance between notes.

Harmonizing Music

Intervals allow musicians to create harmonies, making melodies sound fuller and richer.

Improving Ear Training

Musicians practice interval recognition to identify notes just by listening, which helps with composition and improvisation.

Lesson Conclusion

Intervals are the foundation of all music. They determine the structure of melodies, harmonies, and chords, making them essential for any musician to understand. Whether you are playing piano, guitar, or any other instrument, knowing intervals will help you navigate the keyboard, recognize notes by ear, and compose music with confidence.
