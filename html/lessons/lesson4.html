Lesson 4

Semitone & Tones

In our previous lesson, we understand what an interval is and we also look at some intervals but we did not say much about semitones and tones.

What is a semitone?

A semitone (also called a half step) is the smallest interval or distance between two notes. Let's say you go from D to D#. The interval between the two notes is a semitone. Thus we can say that a semitone is an interval too but the shortest interval between two notes

But what is a tone?

A tone (also called a whole step) is made up of two semitones. Let's say you go from D to D#, that is a semitone. Then let's say you have another semitone from D# to E. Now from D to E is a tone. The interval is as a tone.

How is this Important

We use the combination of tones and semitones to play or make any posible melody. For instance;

  • We use semitones and tones to build scales like how we have W W H W W W H for the major scale(W = whole-step, H = half-step) which we use in turn to create melodies. See that in our next lesson
  • We use semitones and tones to build chords
